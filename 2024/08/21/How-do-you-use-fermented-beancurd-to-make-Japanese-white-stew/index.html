How do you use fermented beancurd to make Japanese white stew

There was once a hot debate on whether we can regard fermented Tofu as a kind of Chinese-like cheese. Although I don’t know the result of the debate, I know that fermented tofu, or “Furu”, is salty and savoury, and can provide creamy feelings. And I will regard everything that can be melted and provide creamy texture as cheese. So it is prompting for me to try that out by replacing whipping cream with Fermented tofu curbs in Japanese white stews.

Ingredients:

  • Button Mushrooms 100g
  • Chicken leg quarter - 2pcs, deboned(but keep the bones with you)
  • Chinese pea/Pea - 100g
  • Onion - 1/2 pcs (Both purple and white are OK, but I perfer white because they are more mild.)
  • Furu Curbs - 5pcs
  • pepper, to taste
  • Garlic - 2 Cloves
  • whipping cream - 40ml

Cooking process.

  • Remove the fiber at the edge of the pea beans and cut it off from the middle. Slice the button mushroom into 1/3 cm-thick. Chop the onion into strips. cut the garlic cloves into slides.
  • Put about one third of onion strips into a large bowl. Cut the chicken bones into small pieces to make sure the bone marrow can be in contact with water and add it into the bowl. Add about 200ml of water and put it into a microwave owen and cook for about 6mins in second-highest power level.
  • Add vegetarian oil into a frying pan, and stir-fry the garlic slides until they release fragrance. Add in the onion and keep stir-frying until the become soft, which will take about around 5 mins.
  • Use a sieve to pour the “cheating” chicken broth into the pan, then stir in the whipping cream. Put in the peas and mushrooms. If you like, you can also add carrots. Let it stew for 10 mins, take care of the thickness of the pot and adjust gas flux accordingly to avoid overcook.
  • Stir in the Furu curbs, and repeatly press it until it is fully dissolved in the soup. Continue heating it until reaching the desired thickness by steaming out the water.
  • Taste and add salt accordingly, and serve with rice.

Notes:

  • The chicken broth is really shabby. If you have enough time (and you should have if you don’t play video games until you are hungry), the better approach would be place the chicken on a aluminium foil and bake it in \(200^\circ C\) for 8 minutes or until golden brown, then put it in the microwave oven for a more complex aroma. Scrap the remainings on the foil to the soup can add flavor, but please keep the oil from it to avoid turmid(but that is not very important since the soup is already not that transparent)
  • You may add any vegetables you like in the recipe, like broccoli, cauliflower or potato. Add more water(or chicken broth) if you added the potato and stir more often, since the starch in potato will make the soup too thick that it is easy to burnt.
